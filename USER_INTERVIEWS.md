Interview 1

Name: Sobiya
Role: Doctor
Date: 2026-05-11

Direct quotes:

"We use multiple AI tools but never know if we are overspending."
"Most of our tools are bought by different departments without coordination."
"We need a simple way to understand where money is wasted."

Most surprising thing they said:
They didn’t have a centralized system to track AI tool subscriptions across departments, even in a clinical environment where budgets are strict.

What it changed about your design:
Added a unified dashboard view that aggregates all tools across users and highlights redundant or overlapping subscriptions.

Interview 2

Name: Rishab Sharma
Role: Small Startup
Date: 2026-05-12

Direct quotes:

"We keep adding AI tools but rarely remove old ones."
"Half the team uses ChatGPT, others use Claude, and we don’t know what’s actually cheaper."
"A comparison tool would help us decide quickly."

Most surprising thing they said:
Even technical teams don’t actively optimize AI spending—they just accumulate tools over time.

What it changed about your design:
Introduced per-tool optimization suggestions with clear “switch / downgrade / keep” actions instead of just showing cost breakdowns.

Interview 3

Name: Adnan
Role: Freelance 
Date: 2026-05-13

Direct quotes:

"I subscribe to tools only when I need them for a project, then forget to cancel."
"Sometimes I don’t even realize I’m paying for unused AI tools."
"A reminder system for unused subscriptions would be very useful."

Most surprising thing they said:
Individual freelancers have higher subscription waste than teams because there is no billing oversight or accountability.

What it changed about your design:
Added a “stale subscription detection” feature and alerts for tools that have low or no usage over time, especially for individual users.
